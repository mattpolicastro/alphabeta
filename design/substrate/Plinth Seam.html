The substrate: strategy ⇄ evidence, joined at the seam

Job one — discernment. "The work" is mostly execution: identify customers, update a dashboard, develop a tree structure. But an A/B test is a claim about the world. The seam's first move is recognizing which items are bets and pulling only those across — the rest stay tasks in the strategy layer.

Job two — flow down. Crossing isn't a copy-paste. The goal's metric and 30%→80% target seed the bet — its expectation and fold-if inherit the strategic "why," so you never articulate a bet from a blank page.

Job three — flow back up. The bet's revisit verdict gates the sequence: a win unlocks "send to all" and nudges the goal; a loss withholds the rollout entirely.

On calibration, your worry: this delivers the "avoided a bad release" value as outcome-as-gate, not outcome-as-grade — the bad rollout shows up as a step that quietly never fired. No win-rate scoreboard to demoralize the team or invite gaming. (A real track-record view stays a later opt-in.)
