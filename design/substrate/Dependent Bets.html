a lost prior prunes the branch

Bets aren't islands — they form sequences where each one's premise rests on the last. Resolve them in order and the verdict propagates: a win unlocks the next; a loss prunes everything downstream before you run it. That pruning is the value — you don't spend on experiments built on a result that didn't hold.

conditional progression
Sequences of bets — the verdict propagates

Each bet rests on the prior's result. A win unlocks the next; a loss prunes everything premised on it — before you spend the traffic and the weeks. The dangerous corner isn't the mid-chain loss; it's ignoring it.

So a pruned bet can be overridden — "run anyway" — but it's flagged as running on an unproven premise, and logged beside the chain. Nudge, not gate, all the way down.

This is the "avoided a bad release" value again, structural rather than scored: the saved bets show up as branches that simply never ran — no win-rate scoreboard required.
