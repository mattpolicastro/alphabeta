## Motivating question
Kickoff thread #1 from the handoff: *alternative decompositions of the setup phase.* The
existing prototype collapsed setup into a four-step wizard (intent → feasibility → criteria →
commit). Was four the right number? Was a wizard even the right container?

## What it explored
Three structurally distinct cuts, presented as tabs so they could be compared:
- **A — Dissolve the wizard.** One canvas; feasibility as an always-on sidebar, not a gated
  step; progressive commit. *Bet: the wizard is the wrong container.*
- **B — Two epistemic acts.** Should we? (intent + feasibility fused) → Lock it (criteria +
  commit). *Bet: setup is two judgments, not four steps.*
- **C — Twin Can/Should surfaces.** Five beats; "Can" (the math) and "Should" (the prudence)
  split into separate screens. *Bet: the can/should distinction earns two literal surfaces.*

## Key bets
Each cut moved a different lever: commit placement, whether feasibility is a gate, and
insertion-moment density. The annotation panel named the bet and its cost for each.

## Feedback that motivated the next step
> *"These actually map to levels of complexity/expertise I'd been exploring — A for experts,
> C for neophytes."*

That reframed everything: the three cuts weren't a menu to choose from, they were **one
axis**. → led to the Register Dial.
