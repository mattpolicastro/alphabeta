why now

The wizard's sin is implying setup is sequential. It isn't — you reason about expectation and feasibility together, glancing back and forth. Putting feasibility in a fixed sidebar matches how the judgment actually feels.

Discipline here is ambient: the cost of running is in your peripheral vision from the first keystroke, not behind a "Next" button.

structural bet

The wizard is the wrong container. Setup is one room, not four doors.

cost: no forced sequence means weaker pre-registration discipline — easy to lock with criteria half-thought-through. The progressive commit has to carry all the rigor.
where I landed: B is the truest to "can vs should" — it makes prudence the primary seam and commits in two honest acts. C is the most diagnostic for the stats-gap persona, because it physically separates "the math says X" from "but consider Y". A is the riskiest bet (weakest pre-registration discipline) but the most honest about how setup actually feels.
