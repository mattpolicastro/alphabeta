## Motivating question
If A/B/C are an expertise axis, turn them into a dial. But the reviewer flagged the
"heebie-jeebies": a surface that *morphs* threatens UX consistency and bakes in hidden
assumptions about the user.

## What it explored
The reframe that defused the worry: **two different things were changing across A/B/C —
structure and narration.** Morphing *structure* is the disorienting part. Flexing *narration*
is safe. So: fix the bones, flex only the talk.

One skeleton (intent · feasibility · criteria · commit), and the register —
**Scaffolded / Standard / Ambient** — only changes how much the discipline layer *narrates*.
An **"outline what the dial touches"** toggle proves it: narration lights up terracotta, the
committed spine stays green and still.

## Key bets
- The register is **user-owned, reversible, and never touches the commitments** — a wrong
  setting is over- or under-talkative, never destructive. (Kills the hidden-assumption worry.)
- Default toward *more* explanation — over-narrating is safe; under-narrating loses the
  discipline. (Also closes the bad-faith "dial away the scaffolding" loophole.)
