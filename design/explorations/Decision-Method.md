## Motivating question
Could ⍺lphaβeta be a multi-method decision-support tool rather than an A/B tool — and would
that resolve the "diagonal user" (deep domain knowledge, shaky statistics)?

## What it explored
**The decision is the spine; the method is a module that plugs into it.** Swap the instrument
(A/B, quasi-experiment, observational, holdback) and the decision journal — question,
expectation, the actions you'll take — never moves. Only the feasibility surface and the
running discipline change.

A **"show what the method supplies"** toggle marks the boundary: green = your decision
(constant), terracotta = method-supplied. The one element that legitimately crosses the line
is the **"how you'll know" evidence bar** — method-aware, but living inside the decision.

## Key bets
- The diagonal user's two fluencies (domain × stats) map onto **two layers**, not one slider:
  author the decision (your strength), lean on the module for the math (your gap).
- The register dial from iteration 2 **relocates** to the method layer — that's where the
  stats narration belongs.
- **Method selection is itself an insertion moment** — arguably the first one.
