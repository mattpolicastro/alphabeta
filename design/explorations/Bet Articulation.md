## Motivating question
How does a loose idea become a disciplined, *falsifiable bet* — with low friction? This is the
sharpest moment for the adjacent-expert persona: turning a strong domain belief into a bet
(direction, magnitude, confidence, and *what would change my mind*) is exactly the stats gap.

## What it explored
Three personalities for the front door, as tabs:
- **The wager sentence** — discipline hidden in the blanks of one readable sentence.
- **Dump & reflect** — say it freely; the tool reflects the bet it heard and *flags the gap*
  (the missing falsifier).
- **Triage first** — drop the bet on a confidence × reversibility grid; its position sets the
  downstream rigor.

A shared spine across all three: **falsifiability is the one thing insisted on** — "a bet you
can't lose isn't a bet."

## Feedback that resolved the design
> *"Wager sentence is good structure but too demanding for first contact (maybe an advanced /
> experienced-user mode). Dump & reflect feels closer — maybe dump+reflect as first contact,
> then help structure into the wager as step 2. Triage-first will lead to analysis paralysis.
> Falsifiability is critical, and I like the natural-language-first approach."*

## What carried forward (into `../Bet Front Door.html`)
- **Dump → reflect → sharpen-into-wager** as one fused flow; natural language first, the
  wager as *destination* not entry.
- The wager sentence kept as an **express lane** for experienced users.
- Triage **cut** (analysis paralysis).
- The reflection **scans the dump for a real falsifier** and won't call it a bet until it can
  lose. Later: a **mechanism/theory** field separated from confidence (if / then / because).
